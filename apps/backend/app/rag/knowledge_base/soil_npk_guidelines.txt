Title: Soil NPK and pH Guidelines

Balanced nitrogen, phosphorus and potassium application improves yield and input efficiency.
Typical pH range for many field crops is 6.0 to 7.5, though crop-specific tolerance differs.
Low phosphorus can reduce root development and early growth.
Potassium supports stress tolerance and grain quality.
Split fertilizer dosing and soil-test-based application are preferred to blanket recommendations.
Combine organic matter with chemical inputs to improve long-term soil health.

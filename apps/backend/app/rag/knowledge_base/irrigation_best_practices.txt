Title: Irrigation Best Practices for Indian Farms

Irrigation should be planned based on crop stage, soil moisture and rainfall forecast.
For paddy and sugarcane, avoid continuous overwatering; use alternate wetting and drying where feasible.
For wheat and pulses, critical stages include crown root initiation, flowering and grain filling.
Use morning or evening irrigation windows to reduce evaporation loss.
Drip and sprinkler systems improve water-use efficiency compared to flood irrigation.
When rainfall above 5 mm is expected in the next 24 hours, postpone non-critical irrigation cycles.
